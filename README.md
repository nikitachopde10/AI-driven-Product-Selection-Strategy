## 📌 Executive Summary
A mid-sized e-commerce apparel firm operates under a strict **fixed advertising budget**, allowing it to promote only a subset of its 331-product catalogue each period. 

The core managerial challenge: **Which products should receive an advertising slot to maximize total purchase revenue?** Wasting ad spend on products that will not sell (False Positives) directly erodes ROI. This project builds a principled, repeatable AI pipeline to identify the highest-potential products before launching a campaign, translating machine learning probabilities directly into expected revenue.

### 🏆 Key Business Outcomes
* **2x Lift in Sales:** The winning model (XGBoost built on LLM-extracted features) achieves approximately a **2x lift** over random product selection at the top-10 level.
* **Maximized ROI:** By successfully ranking products by expected purchase likelihood, the firm can expect twice as many successful purchases on the exact same fixed ad budget.
* **Trust & Auditability:** Every AI prediction is backed by a written justification generated by the LLM, making the "black box" transparent and trustworthy for non-technical stakeholders and marketing managers.

---

## ⚙️ Methodology & AI Strategies
To solve this, I designed and compared multiple predictive strategies, bridging the gap between Generative AI and classical Machine Learning:

1. **Part A — LLM Feature Engineering:** Leveraged `gemini-2.0-flash` to parse messy, unstructured product titles and extract 9 structured attributes (material, fit, style, etc.) via strict JSON schema prompting.
2. **Part B — Embedding-Based ML:** Used `gemini-embedding-001` to generate 3072-dimensional semantic vectors of product titles, applying **PCA (Principal Component Analysis)** to reduce dimensions to 20 to prevent overfitting before feeding them into XGBoost/Random Forest.
3. **Part C — ML Benchmarks:** Trained classical models (Logistic Regression, Random Forest, XGBoost) on the LLM-extracted features, thoroughly tuned via 5-fold Stratified Cross-Validation.
4. **Part D — Pure LLM Prediction:** Tested "Zero-Shot" and "Few-Shot" prompting, deploying the LLM directly as an autonomous buyer agent to estimate purchase probabilities without traditional ML training.

---

## 📊 Decision Framework & Evaluation
Success in this context is **not** about maximizing baseline accuracy (given the 32% purchase rate imbalance). It is about **ranking quality** and **revenue capture**. 

Models were evaluated on **ROC-AUC**, and predictions were translated into business logic using the following optimization formula:

> **Expected Revenue = Price × P(purchase)**

Products were ranked entirely by this metric, guaranteeing that the selected ad slots target the highest absolute return per advertising dollar.

## 🧠 The Strategic Role of Generative AI
While advanced LLMs like Gemini were tested for direct prediction, their most indispensable business value came from:
* **Feature Extraction:** Operating as an automated data-entry engine to structure messy text data.
* **Interpretability Layer:** Generating human-readable rationale to explain *why* a specific product should or shouldn't be advertised.
